Journey to UCF

Mark Pido

How did I get here?

Being raised in Tampa most of my life, I didn't consider moving to Orlando and attending UCF as a possibility. While I did get accepted during the application process, my first choice was USF due to money purposes. However, my brother wanted to go to UCF as a music major, and events leaded up to me following him and transferring to UCF.

Decisions

I started UCF a little ahead in credits due to taking college courses in high school, I honestly didn't really know what I wanted to major in so I had less time to think about what to major in while I complete my general courses. Growing up using technology my whole life, I figured it would make sense to major in either IT or Computer Science, something that pays well and could be considered a stable career if I were to stick with it in the long run.

IT/Computer Science

Graduating high school in 2020, I was apart of the many students who started their college journey online during COVID. Starting as an IT major, I thought the classes were a little tough, even as beginner coding classes, but having little coding experience, I thought it was pretty tough. I don't think having classes via Zoom helped a lot, as I couldn't learn too much. There was a sudden switch throughout this time period where I thought majoring in Computer Science rather than IT would be better for me in the future, so I decided to switch majors. This is when I was finally able to attend classes in-person, and while I did manage to get by as an IT major, I realized Computer Science is a lot more rigorous in terms of work load and decided it didn't fit my lifestyle nor click in general.

Digital Media

Moving into 2022, I finally switched my major to Digital Media on the web design track. For context, I always loved to do anything related to design; doing some research, I found that interface design really interested me, so I decided to pursue this major instead. While I'm not too deep into the track, I definitely found that learning web design was more interesting and something I found enjoyable and satisfying.

What's next

The journey so far has been a lot of ups and downs, I hoped to graduate within a certain time frame, but ultimately realized it's a lot better to take your time to find and learn something more enjoyable and can consider a career in the future. I'm hoping to stick through with the major and find an interface design job.
